### STCG (Short Term Capital Gain)

Profit from selling equity within 1 year.

### LTCG (Long Term Capital Gain)

Profit from selling equity after 1 year.

### SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

Regular investment in mutual funds.

### XIRR

Extended Internal Rate of Return used for irregular cash flows.

### NAV

Net Asset Value of a mutual fund.

### AUM

Assets Under Management of a fund.

### ETF

Exchange Traded Fund that tracks an index.

### Diversification

Spreading investments across sectors to reduce risk.

### Equity

Ownership in a company via shares.

### Bond

Debt instrument where investor lends money.

### Yield

Return generated from an investment.

### Volatility

Measure of price fluctuation.

### Market Cap

Total value of a company’s shares.

### P/E Ratio

Price to Earnings ratio.

### Liquidity

Ease of buying/selling an asset.

### CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)

The average annual growth rate of an investment over a period of time.

### Dividend Yield

Annual dividend expressed as a percentage of the stock price.

### Alpha

Excess return of an investment compared to a benchmark index.

### Beta

Measure of a stock’s volatility relative to the market.

### Expense Ratio

Annual fee charged by mutual funds or ETFs as a percentage of assets.

### Asset Allocation

Distribution of investments across asset classes like equity, debt, etc.

### Rebalancing

Adjusting portfolio weights to maintain desired allocation.

### Bull Market

A market condition where prices are rising.

### Bear Market

A market condition where prices are falling.

### Risk Appetite

The level of risk an investor is willing to take.

### Capital Gain

Profit from selling an asset at a higher price than purchase.

### Debt Fund

A mutual fund investing in fixed-income securities.

### Equity Fund

A mutual fund investing primarily in stocks.

### Index Fund

A fund that replicates a market index like NIFTY 50.

### Blue Chip Stocks

Large, well-established companies with stable performance.

### Mid Cap

Companies with medium market capitalization.

### Small Cap

Companies with smaller market capitalization and higher risk.

### Liquidity Risk

Risk of not being able to sell an asset quickly.

### Credit Risk

Risk that a borrower may default on obligations.

### Inflation

Increase in prices reducing purchasing power.

### Hedging

Strategy used to reduce potential losses.

### Portfolio Turnover

Frequency of buying and selling within a portfolio.

### NAV Growth

Increase in Net Asset Value over time.

### Yield to Maturity (YTM)

Expected return if a bond is held till maturity.

### Face Value

Nominal value of a bond or stock.

### Market Order

An order to buy/sell immediately at current price.

### Limit Order

An order to buy/sell at a specified price.

### Sector Rotation

Shifting investments between sectors based on market trends.

### Drawdown

Decline from a portfolio’s peak value.

### Sharpe Ratio

Measure of risk-adjusted return.

### Diversified Portfolio

A portfolio spread across multiple sectors/assets.

### Overweight

Higher allocation to a sector compared to benchmark.

### Underweight

Lower allocation compared to benchmark.
